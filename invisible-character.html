What is an Invisible Character?

An invisible character (sometimes called an empty character or blank space) is a special Unicode symbol that represents a space without actually being a traditional "spacebar" character. Because standard spaces are automatically stripped or ignored by many apps, you need these special unicode characters to trick the system into displaying nothing.

Where can you use an Empty Space?

  • WhatsApp & iMessage: Send completely empty, blank messages to your friends to confuse them.
  • Instagram Bios: Use invisible characters to force line breaks and center your text perfectly in your IG bio.
  • Gaming (Fortnite, PUBG, Free Fire): Create an invisible username or a clan name with blank spaces that the game normally wouldn't allow.
  • Discord: Set your nickname or server channel names to be completely blank.

Why not just use the Spacebar?

Modern applications are designed to trim excess whitespace. If you type a regular space into a chat box and hit send, the app recognizes it as an empty input and won't let you send the message. By using a Unicode symbol like the Braille Pattern Blank (U+2800) or Hangul Filler (U+3164), the application thinks you are sending a real letter, even though it is completely invisible to the human eye.
